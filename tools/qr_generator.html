The Standard for Secure, Static QR Codes

Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes

Most free tools on the internet generate Dynamic QR Codes. These codes do not actually contain your website URL; instead, they contain a shortened tracking link owned by the generator company. This allows them to track analytics, but it also allows them to disable your QR code and demand a subscription fee after it has been printed on your business cards.

The SilenVault engine strictly generates Static QR Codes. The exact text or URL you type is mathematically encoded directly into the black and white squares. Because there is no middle-man server tracking the link, these codes are completely free, incredibly fast to scan, and will literally never expire.

Error Correction Levels (ECL)

QR codes feature built-in redundancy known as the Reed-Solomon error correction algorithm. This ensures that even if part of the printed code is torn, smudged, or covered, the scanner can still recover the data.

  • Level L (Low): Can sustain ~7% damage. Best for massive URLs where you want the code to look less dense and "cleaner."
  • Level M (Medium): Can sustain ~15% damage. The standard setting for digital displays and general printing.
  • Level H (High): Can sustain ~30% damage. Essential if you plan to overlay a custom logo in the center of the code, or if the code will be printed in a harsh physical environment.
