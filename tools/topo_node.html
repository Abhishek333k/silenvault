How to Use the Elevation & Distance Calculator

Traditional maps only calculate "2D Ground Distance," completely ignoring the physical terrain, hills, and valleys. This utility uses open-source Topographic APIs to extract the exact Above Sea Level (ASL) elevation of map coordinates to mathematically resolve the True 3D Slant Distance (Hypotenuse) and the angle of incline between two points.

Step 1

Use the Layers button (top-right of the map) to select Satellite Mode or Street Mode. Zoom into your target area.

Step 2

Click the map to drop Point A. The system will instantly fetch its exact Sea Level elevation.

Step 3

Click to drop Point B. The engine calculates the true 3D distance, height difference, and slope angle.

Practical Engineering Applications

  • Borewell & Submersible Pump Planning When drawing water from a valley to a house on a hill, you cannot measure the terrain with a tape measure. This tool calculates the exact Slant Distance to tell you precisely how much PVC pipe and wiring you need, while the Height Difference (Δh) dictates the "Head Capacity" (HP) your submersible motor requires.
  • RF Wireless Line-of-Sight (LoS) Essential for Point-to-Point Wi-Fi or radio installations. By checking the elevation angle and terrain height between two buildings or mountains, engineers can calculate if the Fresnel Zone will be obstructed by the physical earth.
  • Construction & Trail Logistics Calculate the physical length of fencing, power lines, or hiking trails over steep mountainsides where flat 2D maps fail to account for vertical traversal.

Mathematical Trigonometry Engine

  • 1. Surface Distance (d): Computed via Haversine Formula mapping the curvature of the Earth.
  • 2. Height Delta (Δh):
    | Elevation_B - Elevation_A |
  • 3. 3D Slant Distance (Hypotenuse):
    √(d² + Δh²)
  • 4. Elevation Angle (Incline):
    arctan(Δh / d) × (180 / π)
